I write about software development, open source, and digital freedom. This blog exists because I'm tired of how Big Tech murders user freedom through surveillance capitalism, vendor lock-in, and corporate enclosure of the digital commons. They've taken something beautiful—open collaboration and shared knowledge—and turned it into extraction, control, and exploitation.

I write for developers who want to build tools that empower users, not exploit them. I advocate for free software as a moral imperative, open protocols over walled gardens, and privacy as a human right. No corporate doublespeak, no "ecosystem" bullshit, no pretending surveillance is "personalization"—just honest thinking about software, freedom, and who actually benefits from the systems we build. If you want clear explanations and give a damn about user freedom, stick around.
